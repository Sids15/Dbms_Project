THE HISTORY OF FORMULA 1

From humble beginnings to a global spectacle

F1 THROUGH THE DECADES

1950s

The Birth of Formula 1

The inaugural Formula 1 World Championship was held in 1950, with the first race taking place at Silverstone in the United Kingdom. Giuseppe Farina became the first World Champion, driving an Alfa Romeo.

7 Races in the first F1 season
1960s

The Era of British Teams

The 1960s saw the rise of British racing teams like Lotus, BRM, and Cooper. The mid-engine layout became standard, and innovations in aerodynamics began to reshape car design.

1962 First monocoque chassis introduced by Lotus
1970s

The Revolution of Aerodynamics

The 1970s brought revolutionary ground-effect aerodynamics, which dramatically increased downforce. This decade saw legendary rivalries, including Hunt vs. Lauda, and the dominance of drivers like Jackie Stewart.

1977 Lotus introduces ground effect with the Lotus 78
1980s

The Turbo Era

The 1980s were defined by turbocharged engines producing over 1,000 horsepower. This decade witnessed the emergence of legends like Ayrton Senna and Alain Prost, and the dominance of McLaren and Williams.

1,400 Peak horsepower from BMW turbo engines in qualifying
1990s

The Digital Revolution

The 1990s saw the implementation of advanced electronics and driver aids before many were banned. The decade was marked by the fierce rivalry between Senna and Prost, and later the rise of Michael Schumacher.

1994 Major safety improvements following Ayrton Senna's tragic accident
2000s

The Schumacher Era

The 2000s were dominated by Michael Schumacher and Ferrari, who won five consecutive championships from 2000 to 2004. This period also saw significant advances in car safety and reliability.

5 Consecutive World Championships for Ferrari (2000-2004)
2010s

Hybrid Power Units

The 2010s introduced hybrid power units, combining traditional internal combustion engines with energy recovery systems. The decade witnessed the dominance of Red Bull Racing and Mercedes, and the emergence of Lewis Hamilton as a record-breaking champion.

2014 Introduction of hybrid V6 turbo power units
2020s

New Regulations & New Era

The 2020s began with revolutionary new regulations aimed at creating closer racing and more overtaking opportunities. Cost caps were implemented to level the playing field between teams, and a new generation of drivers emerged.

2022 Major regulation changes with ground effect returning
