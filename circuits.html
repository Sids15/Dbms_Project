Canadian Grand Prix

Date: June 9

Location: Montreal

Length: 4.361 km

Laps: 70

Held at Circuit Gilles Villeneuve, famous for tight chicanes and high-speed straights.

History: First hosted in 1978, the Canadian GP became iconic due to Gilles Villeneuve's victory in its debut year. The circuit was renamed in his honor after his tragic passing.

Famous Moments: Jenson Button’s legendary 2011 win in changing weather is one of F1’s greatest comebacks. The "Wall of Champions" has claimed many elite drivers.

Fun Fact: The track is located on a man-made island originally built for the 1967 Expo and 1976 Olympics.

Abu Dhabi Grand Prix

Date: December 8

Location: Yas Marina

Length: 5.281 km

Laps: 58

Season finale at twilight, blending day and night racing drama.

History: Introduced in 2009, it's the first F1 race to start in daylight and finish under floodlights.

Famous Moments: The 2021 showdown between Verstappen and Hamilton was one of the most controversial and intense championship finishes in history.

Fun Fact: Drivers race under a luxury hotel that changes colors throughout the race, making it visually iconic.

Bahrain Grand Prix

Date: March 2

Location: Sakhir

Length: 5.412 km

Laps: 57

The season opener under the lights, showcasing intense desert action.

History: First hosted in 2004 and became a night race in 2014 to beat the desert heat.

Famous Moments: Known for wheel-to-wheel battles and dramatic strategies.

Fun Fact: The 2020 Sakhir GP layout was an ultra-fast oval configuration.

Saudi Arabian Grand Prix

Date: March 9

Location: Jeddah

Length: 6.174 km

Laps: 50

One of the fastest street circuits in the world, lit entirely by LEDs.

History: Debuted in 2021 with narrow barriers and high speeds.

Famous Moments: 2021 featured a tense duel between Hamilton and Verstappen.

Fun Fact: Over 600 light poles are used to illuminate the night race.

Australian Grand Prix

Date: March 24

Location: Melbourne

Length: 5.278 km

Laps: 58

Set around Albert Park Lake, offering high-speed racing in a parkland setting.

History: A staple in F1 since 1996, often the season opener before Bahrain took over.

Famous Moments: 2002 saw a massive first-corner crash involving multiple cars.

Fun Fact: Recent layout tweaks made the track faster and more flowing.

Japanese Grand Prix

Date: April 7

Location: Suzuka

Length: 5.807 km

Laps: 53

Legendary figure-eight layout and passionate fans define this iconic track.

History: Hosted title-deciding races in multiple decades.

Famous Moments: Senna vs. Prost in 1989 and 1990 – true F1 drama.

Fun Fact: One of the few circuits to feature a crossover section.

Chinese Grand Prix

Date: April 21

Location: Shanghai

Length: 5.451 km

Laps: 56

Back on the calendar in 2024 with a wide circuit and long straights.

History: Joined the calendar in 2004 and known for variable weather.

Famous Moments: Hamilton's dramatic slide into the gravel in 2007 cost him the title shot.

Fun Fact: The turn 1-2 complex is famous for its snail-shell-like layout.

Miami Grand Prix

Date: May 5

Location: Miami

Length: 5.412 km

Laps: 57

Glamorous American race winding around Hard Rock Stadium.

History: First held in 2022 as part of F1's U.S. expansion.

Famous Moments: Verstappen’s storming drive in 2022 from 3rd to 1st.

Fun Fact: Features a fake marina to evoke Monaco vibes.

Emilia Romagna Grand Prix

Date: May 19

Location: Imola

Length: 4.909 km

Laps: 63

Historic venue surrounded by Ferrari's home crowd and Italian motorsport legacy.

History: Hosted San Marino GPs in the 80s and 90s, returned in 2020.

Famous Moments: 2021 featured a wet-dry thriller with red flags and chaos.

Fun Fact: The circuit layout honors the memory of Ayrton Senna and Roland Ratzenberger.

Monaco Grand Prix

Date: May 26

Location: Monte Carlo

Length: 3.337 km

Laps: 78

The crown jewel of F1 with tight streets, yachts, and glitz.

History: Part of F1 since 1950, it’s a driver’s favorite for prestige.

Famous Moments: Senna’s dominance and crashes in the narrow streets.

Fun Fact: It’s so tight, overtaking is nearly impossible—qualifying is everything.

Location: Montreal

Length: 4.361 km

High-speed straights and tight chicanes on a semi-street circuit.

History: Home to dramatic wet races and the 'Wall of Champions'.

Spanish Grand Prix

Date: June 23

Location: Barcelona

Length: 4.657 km

Laps: 66

A favorite for testing, with a variety of corners and technical challenges.

History: Held at the Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya since 1991.

Famous Moments: Verstappen’s maiden win in 2016 after a Mercedes clash.

Fun Fact: Teams know this track better than any other due to preseason testing.

Austrian Grand Prix

Date: June 30

Location: Spielberg

Length: 4.318 km

Laps: 71

Short, fast, and scenic, nestled in the Styrian mountains.

History: Red Bull revived the circuit in 2014 after years of absence.

Famous Moments: Norris defending against Hamilton in 2021 thrilled fans.

Fun Fact: It's one of the shortest lap times on the calendar.

British Grand Prix

Date: July 7

Location: Silverstone

Length: 5.891 km

Laps: 52

Birthplace of F1 and home to some of the most passionate fans.

History: Hosted the very first F1 race in 1950.

Famous Moments: Hamilton’s comeback drive in 2021 after a clash with Verstappen.

Fun Fact: Known for Maggotts-Becketts—one of the best corner sequences in motorsport.

Hungarian Grand Prix

Date: July 21

Location: Budapest

Length: 4.381 km

Laps: 70

Tight and twisty, often compared to Monaco without the walls.

History: A mainstay since 1986 and often unpredictable due to summer storms.

Famous Moments: Alonso’s maiden win in 2003 and Ocon’s 2021 upset victory.

Fun Fact: Overtaking is tough, making qualifying crucial.

Belgian Grand Prix

Date: July 28

Location: Spa-Francorchamps

Length: 7.004 km

Laps: 44

Legendary high-speed rollercoaster through the Ardennes Forest.

History: Historic, fast, and dangerous—Spa is a fan and driver favorite.

Famous Moments: Schumacher’s debut in 1991 and countless Eau Rouge battles.

Fun Fact: Weather can change in different parts of the track within minutes.

Dutch Grand Prix

Date: August 25

Location: Zandvoort

Length: 4.259 km

Laps: 72

Coastal rollercoaster with steep banking and passionate home crowd.

History: Returned in 2021 after a 36-year absence to honor Dutch hero Max Verstappen.

Famous Moments: Verstappen’s dominant wins electrified orange-clad fans.

Fun Fact: The circuit has a unique banked corner at Turn 3 that challenges even seasoned drivers.

Italian Grand Prix

Date: September 1

Location: Monza

Length: 5.793 km

Laps: 53

The "Temple of Speed" where history and horsepower collide.

History: One of the oldest tracks, Monza has been on the calendar since 1950.

Famous Moments: Gasly’s 2020 win stunned the paddock and fans alike.

Fun Fact: Tifosi (Ferrari fans) bring unmatched energy to the podium celebrations.

Azerbaijan Grand Prix

Date: September 15

Location: Baku

Length: 6.003 km

Laps: 51

Fast street circuit with castle walls and chaos guaranteed.

History: Joined in 2016 and quickly earned a reputation for unpredictable races.

Famous Moments: Bottas’ heartbreaking puncture in 2018; Vettel vs. Hamilton clash in 2017.

Fun Fact: Home to the longest flat-out section on the calendar.

Singapore Grand Prix

Date: September 22

Location: Marina Bay

Length: 4.928 km

Laps: 61

The original night race with unforgiving walls and humid heat.

History: First held in 2008 and known for its street circuit brutality.

Famous Moments: The “Crashgate” scandal and numerous safety cars over the years.

Fun Fact: Drivers lose up to 3kg due to extreme humidity and heat.

United States Grand Prix

Date: October 20

Location: Austin

Length: 5.513 km

Laps: 56

COTA combines corners from legendary circuits and Texas flair.

History: Hosted since 2012 with strong fan support and fun racing.

Famous Moments: Hamilton’s many wins and Kimi’s fan-favorite 2018 victory.

Fun Fact: Turn 1’s uphill blind apex is a unique challenge in F1.

Mexican Grand Prix

Date: October 27

Location: Mexico City

Length: 4.304 km

Laps: 71

High altitude and a stadium atmosphere like no other.

History: Returned in 2015 with fan-favorite Sergio Pérez energizing the home crowd.

Famous Moments: Verstappen’s early dominance and epic podium scenes in the stadium.

Fun Fact: Engines struggle here due to thin air at 2,200 meters elevation.

Brazilian Grand Prix

Date: November 3

Location: Interlagos

Length: 4.309 km

Laps: 71

Elevation changes, passionate fans, and unpredictable drama.

History: Historic finishes and Senna’s homeland—Brazil is always emotional.

Famous Moments: Hamilton’s 2008 last-corner title win; Verstappen’s wet mastery in 2016.

Fun Fact: Weather often changes mid-race, leading to chaos and surprises.

Las Vegas Grand Prix

Date: November 23

Location: Las Vegas Strip Circuit

Length: 6.120 km

Laps: 50

Neon lights, casinos, and a high-speed dash down the iconic Strip.

History: Revived in 2023 after a controversial debut in the 1980s.

Famous Moments: The 2023 race brought glitz and unpredictability in equal measure.

Fun Fact: Drivers reach over 340 km/h as they blast past famous landmarks like Caesars Palace and the Bellagio.

Qatar Grand Prix

Date: December 1

Location: Lusail

Length: 5.419 km

Laps: 57

A desert night race known for its fast, flowing corners and punishing heat.

History: Joined in 2021 and quickly proved to be physically demanding for drivers.

Famous Moments: Alonso’s 2021 podium and the heat exhaustion chaos of 2023.

Fun Fact: The Lusail International Circuit also hosts MotoGP and endurance racing events.
